:::tip[In short]
The platform depends on the market: in the CIS — **hh.ru** and **Telegram channels**, for abroad — **LinkedIn** (the main channel) plus Indeed/Glassdoor. But the most effective path isn't "cold" applications — it's **networking and referrals**: they fill a huge share of positions, especially ones that never hit public boards.
:::

## Why you need it

You can send 200 applications into the void, or aim precisely where they work. Understanding the channels for your market saves weeks. The job search is also a funnel, and it's worth optimizing.

## CIS

- **hh.ru** — the main platform, the most analyst openings. Set up filters and a saved search.
- **Telegram channels** — channels and bots with DA jobs (often appear before hh, including startups and remote). A very lively channel in the CIS.
- **getmatch, "Habr Career"** — IT-oriented, often with salary ranges stated.

## Abroad

- **LinkedIn** — the main tool. A filled-out profile = your "online resume"; recruiters search and find through it. Almost mandatory for abroad.
- **Indeed, Glassdoor** — job aggregators (Glassdoor also shows company reviews and salaries) — especially US/UK.
- Niche boards and relocation Telegram/Slack communities.

## Networking — the strongest channel

:::tip[Most positions are filled via referrals]
A referral from an employee sharply raises your odds: your resume is reviewed carefully, bypassing some filters. How to use it:

- maintain **LinkedIn**: post about projects, comment — you get noticed;
- attend analytics meetups/webinars, talk to people in the industry;
- don't hesitate to ask for a **referral** from acquaintances at a company;
- many positions never reach public boards — they're filled "through connections".
:::

## Agencies and recruiters

External recruiters and agencies sometimes reach out to candidates themselves (especially via LinkedIn). Useful, but remember: they work for the employer. It's an extra channel, not the main one.

## How not to burn out

The search is a funnel: applications → screenings → interviews → offers. Rejections at each step are normal (recall [funnels and conversion](/en/08-product-analytics/03-funnels/)). Track your conversion: many applications and zero screenings — the problem is the [resume](/en/12-career/01-resume/); reaching the tech interview and failing — improve your [preparation](/en/12-career/05-technical-interview/).

## Practice tasks

<details>
<summary>1. You're job-hunting abroad. Which channel is the priority and why?</summary>

LinkedIn. It's the main channel of overseas hiring: a filled-out profile works as an online resume, recruiters search and vet candidates through it, and it's convenient for networking and asking for referrals. Indeed/Glassdoor complement it, but LinkedIn is the mandatory minimum for abroad.

</details>

<details>
<summary>2. You sent 150 applications, almost no replies. Where's the problem likely?</summary>

At the funnel's entrance — in the resume and its targeting (or passing the ATS). If a mass of applications doesn't convert into screenings, it's usually not about quantity but the quality/relevance of the resume for the postings. Worth improving the number-backed phrasing and keywords, and adding networking and referrals.

</details>
